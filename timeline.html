Mary Cassatt: Life & Letters Timeline

May 22, 1844 · Birth

Mary Stevenson Cassatt is born in Allegheny City, Pennsylvania (now part of Pittsburgh). She grows up in a wealthy family with a strong emphasis on education and travel.

1860–1865 · Education in Philadelphia

She studies painting at the Academy of the Fine Arts. Frustrated by the limitations placed on female students, she decides to move to Europe.

1866 · Move to Paris

Cassatt travels to France to study the Old Masters and modern techniques. She copies paintings at the Louvre and studies privately with Jean-Léon Gérôme.

1877 · Joins the Impressionists

Edgar Degas invited Cassatt to exhibit with the Impressionists. She became one of the few American women associated with the movement, regularly contributing to their exhibitions. The image below is a portrait of Mary Cassatt painted by Degas titled "Mary Cassatt at the Louvre."

1906 · Exhibits in Paris

Cassatt's paintings are included in at the Durand-Ruel Gallery, reinforcing her influence in the European art market.

1907 · Creates "Young Mother Sewing"

Cassatt deepens her exploration of motherhood and intimacy through one of her last major works.

1915 · Ends Painting Career

Château de Beaufresne, France
Due to failing eyesight caused by diabetes and cataracts, Cassatt stops painting and retreats to her estate.

December 12, 1922 · Letter to Homer Saint-Gaudens

Cassatt reflects on the opinions of Degas and Berthe Morisot about her work, showing her continued engagement with the Impressionist legacy.

1921-1926 · Final Years

By 1915, Cassatt is almost completely blind due to cataracts and diabetes. She stops painting and lives quietly in Château de Beaufresne, near Paris.

June 14, 1926 · Death

Mary Cassatt dies in France at age 82. She is buried at the family vault in Mesnil-Théribus, near her estate.
